DANIELSON FOCUS: Domain 3C — Engaging Students in Learning

Pre-Observation Executive Brief

Instructor: Sarah Jenkins • Lincoln Elementary School

I. Lesson Design & Alignment (Domain 1C)

The lesson aims to teach NGSS 4-LS1-1, explaining how plants process water, carbon dioxide, and sunlight into glucose and oxygen. This abstract chemical transformation is translated into a physical modeling framework, helping 4th-grade minds conceptualize metabolic cycles.

II. Active Cognitive Engagement Strategy (Domain 3C)

Students are placed into collaborative kinetic groups representing the Solar Factory ingredients (The Sun, The Air, The Roots). Students act out the chemical transformation using high-visual aids (flashlights for photons, blue paper for H2O, balloons for CO2) to solidify conceptual energy storage mapping.

III. Accommodations & Inclusions (Domain 1D)

English Language Learners are supported through dual-language vocabulary visual aids (*Chlorophyll / Clorofila*). Neurodivergent students with active IEP accommodations are strategically positioned in central physical groups to provide tactile anchoring during roleplay modeling, eliminating written-response anxiety during key conceptual transition stops.

IV. Formative Assessment & Metrics (Domain 3D)

A paper-based exit slip diagram (The Solar Factory Leaf) checks individual concept mastery at Minute 35, requiring students to label chemical inputs and outputs. Formative benchmark expectation: 85% class mastery.
